> Skeptic's Disclaimer

Let's be blunt: 99% of what flies under the "Web3" banner is speculative grift or governance theater. We keep the 1% that matters—the cryptography, decentralized identifiers, and self-sovereign identity rails—because they are the only functional lockpicks we have against Big Tech's capture of identity and data. Everything else can stay in the hype dumpster.

> Premise

Platforms start generous: low friction, free tiers, lavish support. Once you build on them, incentives flip. Pricing hikes, lock-in, dark patterns, degraded service. The Loot and Scoot approach accepts the inevitability of enshittification and weaponizes it. We take their subsidies, preserve autonomy, and exit before the trap springs.

> The Enshittification Cycle

1. Attract

VC money creates artificial abundance. Free credits, concierge onboarding, API velocity. You are the bait.

2. Extract

Metrics mandate revenue. Terms tighten, data is siloed, support becomes upsell. You are the product.

3. Collapse

Costs rise, trust evaporates, competitors fork the community. The platform withers, but you are already gone.

> Operating Principles

  • Exploit incentives, not people. Take what the subsidy offers while keeping your conscience intact.
  • Autonomy first. Identity, data, and build artifacts stay portable from day zero.
  • Prefer protocols to platforms. Favor standards, open formats, reversible integrations.
  • Automate the exit. Backups, migration scripts, contingency checklists—ready before you need them.
  • Follow the money. Monitor funding rounds, leadership churn, pricing roadmaps for decay signals.
  • Share the maps. Document loopholes, export tools, migration guides for the next crew.

> The Loot & Scoot Playbook

Recon

Profile the platform: ownership, runway, legal terms, export options. Identify the escape hatches early.

Exploit

Max out free quotas, pilot programs, premium support trials. Document every step for future replication.

Fortify

Mirror data to neutral storage. Use SSI wallets, hardware keys, encrypted backups. Bake resilience into infra.

Pivot

Set quarterly exit drills. Test failover to self-hosted or alternative providers. Track switching cost over time.

Broadcast

Publish post-mortems and migration kits. Turn personal escape velocity into community resilience.

Judo Move

Use their generous free-tier bandwidth to serve encrypted data that only you hold the keys to. They pay for the transport; you own the cargo.

> Preferred Toolkit

Web3 is not the promised land—we treat crypto-tech like a getaway car. Standardize on DIDs, portable data formats, and verifiable credentials only because they keep the exit lanes open and the loot under our control. The mission is to extract value and relocate it to resilient communities, not to homestead inside yet another walled garden.

Identity

DIDs, verifiable credentials, passkey-capable auth. Run your own resolver or use federated cooperatives.

Data

Immutable object storage, IPFS pinning, end-to-end encrypted sync. Schema-first design with export scripts.

Comms

Matrix, ActivityPub, email with your own domain. Favor bridges and bots you can self-host.

Compute

Containerized workloads, Terraform state in your S3, CI/CD mirroring. Practice cloud-agnostic deployments.

Finance

Multi-bank treasury, API-based cash sweeps, exit-friendly credit lines. Keep burn flexible and reversible.

> Regenerative Endgame

Looting is the means, regeneration is the end. We redirect the bloated marketing budgets and wasteful VC subsidies we siphon off into public goods, worker-owned infrastructure, and community-maintained commons. The scorecard is measured in mutual aid funds replenished, open tools maintained, and local ecosystems strengthened.

> Exit Signals

"If you feel the friction, the rot already started. Trust your sensors."
  • Mandatory bundling of unrelated paid tiers.
  • Slower support unless you upgrade.
  • API deprecations without migration tooling.
  • Executive churn or activist investor entries.
  • Usage limits shrinking while marketing pushes "pro" plans.
  • Roadmaps pivoting from community features to surveillance and ads.

> The Pledge

We commit to staying light on our feet, suspicious of monopolies, and relentless in preserving agency. We build with reversible moves, never confuse convenience with captivity, and we leave the ladder down for those still inside.

When the cycle resets, we are ready: loot ethically, scoot decisively, and seed the next network with better norms.
