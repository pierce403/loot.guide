> Skeptic's Disclaimer

We won't insult you with hopium. Ninety-nine percent of "Web3" is a carnival of speculative grift, VC cosplay, and exit-liquidity musicals. We keep the one percent that works—the math, the decentralized identifiers, the self-sovereign identity rails—because those are the only real lockpicks against Big Tech's biometric prisons. Everything else can stay burning on the hype barge while we slip through the service entrance.

> Premise

Empires bait the trap with velvet onboarding. Generous credits, concierge engineers, friendly Slack emojis. Then the screws turn: pricing spikes, APIs vanish behind paywalls, terms mutate into shackles. Loot and Scoot accepts the rot as physics. We study their greed, ride the subsidy, siphon the value, and ghost before the clamps close. This isn't paranoia; it's operational hygiene.

> The Enshittification Cycle

1. Attract

VC firehose turns on. Free credits rain down, APIs purr, community managers DM you hearts. You are not the customer—you are the lure they dangle for Wall Street slides.

2. Extract

Growth plateaus, investors sweat. Suddenly "usage-based pricing" replaces your freedom, exports break, and support queues ask for your credit card before your name. Congratulations: you are the product.

3. Collapse

With trust gone and margins starved, the empire cannibalizes itself. Communities fork, regulators sniff around, executives "pursue new challenges." The platform withers, but your convoy already hit the safehouse.

> Operating Principles

  • Exploit incentives, not people. Take the subsidy, leave their users better than you found them.
  • Autonomy first. Identity, data, and artifacts stay portable from day zero—no sentimental attachments.
  • Prefer protocols to platforms. We ride standards, open formats, reversible integrations like stolen bikes.
  • Automate the exit. Backups, migration scripts, contingency drills—rehearsed like a favorite riff.
  • Follow the money. Funding rounds, leadership churn, desperate pricing tweaks—all smell like incoming rot.
  • Share the maps. Every loophole documented is another crew that escapes before the collapse.

> The Loot & Scoot Playbook

Recon

Profile the target like a heist crew. Who owns it, who funds it, which lawyers wrote the terms? Mark the escape hatches on day one.

Exploit

Max out the free tiers, pilot credits, concierge onboarding. Record the ritual so the next operator doesn't trigger the tripwire.

Fortify

Mirror everything to neutral ground. SSI wallets, hardware keys, encrypted stashes. Resilience is a muscle, so train it.

Pivot

Run exit drills like fire alarms. Fail over to your shadow stack, tally switching costs, and keep shaving them down.

Broadcast

Publish the score. Post-mortems, migration kits, redacted war stories. Your escape velocity fuels the next rebellion.

Judo Move

Use their generous free-tier bandwidth to serve encrypted cargo only your crew can unlock. They bankroll the getaway car; you own the loot.

> Preferred Toolkit

Web3 is not a religion; it's the hotwired ride parked out back. We standardize on DIDs, portable data formats, and verifiable credentials because they keep the exit lane clear and the loot in our pockets. We are not here to homestead inside another cult chain—we're using cryptographic getaway cars to move stolen sunlight into community vaults.

Identity

DIDs, verifiable credentials, passkey-capable auth. Run your own resolver or federate with co-ops who answer to users.

Data

Immutable object storage, IPFS pinning, end-to-end encrypted sync. Schemas stay portable, exports stay one command away.

Comms

Matrix, ActivityPub, and email on your own domain. Bridges and bots live on your infra, not theirs.

Compute

Containerized workloads, Terraform state in your S3, CI/CD mirrors on standby. Every workload should ghost to a new cloud overnight.

Finance

Multi-bank treasuries, API cash sweeps, exit-friendly credit lines. Liquidity stays nimble and reversible on command.

> Regenerative Endgame

Looting is the tactic; regeneration is the mission. We funnel their bloated marketing spend and wasted VC billions into public goods, worker-owned infrastructure, and community-run commons. Success is measured in mutual aid funds replenished, open tools maintained, and neighborhoods that no longer need permission slips to thrive.

> Exit Signals

"If you feel friction, the rot already started. Trust your sensors and prep the getaway."
  • Mandatory bundling of unrelated paid tiers—classic lock-in squeeze.
  • Support lines slow-walking you unless you flash a premium badge.
  • API deprecations announced without escape tooling or even a mea culpa.
  • C-suite musical chairs or activist investors promising "efficiency."
  • Usage limits shrinking while sales screams "upgrade or else."
  • Roadmaps pivot from community features to surveillance, ads, and compliance theater.

> The Pledge

We swear to stay light on our feet, allergic to monopolies, and obsessive about collective agency. We build with reversible moves, refuse to confuse convenience with captivity, and keep the ladder lowered for anyone still inside the cage.

When the cycle resets, we are already moving: loot ethically, scoot decisively, and seed the next network with better norms than the last empire ever allowed.
